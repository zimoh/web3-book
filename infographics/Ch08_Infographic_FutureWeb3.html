Key Takeaways
1
AI agents with on-chain wallets can autonomously execute transactions, participate in governance, and manage treasuries — creating new categories of non-human economic actors with legal ambiguity still unresolved.
2
DePIN (Decentralised Physical Infrastructure Networks) tokenises real-world infrastructure — wireless, energy, storage, compute — creating crowdsourced alternatives to capital-intensive centralised incumbents.
3
Responsible innovation in Web3 requires addressing five ethical dimensions: extractive tokenomics, environmental impact, AI agent accountability, financial inclusion trade-offs, and regulatory engagement vs. avoidance.
Glossary
DePIN
Decentralised Physical Infrastructure Network; a protocol using token incentives to coordinate deployment and operation of real-world hardware (sensors, routers, compute, storage).
AI Agent
An autonomous software entity powered by an LLM that can perceive its environment, reason about it, and take actions — including on-chain transactions — without human instruction per action.
Verifiable Compute
Cryptographic proof that a computation was executed correctly without revealing inputs; enables trustless AI inference on decentralised networks — the key primitive for AI × blockchain.
